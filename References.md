# References — starter list

This file collects primary and review literature relevant to dopamine biology, stimulant use and withdrawal, and recovery from dopaminergic dysfunction. Please confirm citations or provide additional references to include; I can add DOIs/URLs or BibTeX entries before committing.

## Suggested core reviews and overviews
- Volkow ND, Fowler JS, Wang G-J — Reviews on dopamine, addiction, and neuroimaging findings.
- Kalivas PW, Volkow ND — Reviews on the neural basis of addiction and relapse.
- Nestler EJ — Molecular mechanisms and long-term plasticity in addiction.
- Robinson TE, Berridge KC — Incentive-sensitization theory of addiction and related work.

## PET and neuroimaging studies
- Studies reporting decreased striatal D2 receptor availability and dopamine function in stimulant users (examples: seminal PET studies by Volkow and colleagues).
- Longitudinal imaging papers on recovery of dopamine markers after abstinence.

## Stimulant withdrawal, anhedonia, and recovery
- Clinical and preclinical reviews of withdrawal syndromes, anhedonia, and behavioral recovery.
- Animal-model studies of dopaminergic recovery and synaptic plasticity after chronic stimulant exposure.

## Biochemistry and pharmacology references
- Textbook chapters / reviews on dopamine synthesis (tyrosine → L-DOPA → dopamine), transporters (DAT), receptors (D1–D5), and catabolic enzymes (MAO, COMT).
- Reviews on precursor supplements, reuptake modulation, and enzyme inhibition — emphasize mechanism summaries over dosing guidance.

## Safety, ethics, and clinical guidance
- Review articles and clinical guidelines addressing the risks of self-administering psychoactive compounds and the importance of clinician supervision.

## How to contribute references
Please submit references as:
- Full citation (authors, title, journal, year)
- DOI or URL
- Short note on relevance (1–2 sentences)
