# Chemical Information and Substance Profiles

This section provides factual, educational information about chemicals and substances relevant to dopamine system function, including both those that affect dopaminergic signaling and precursors or modulators being studied for recovery support.

## Contents

### Dopaminergic Drugs and Mechanisms
- **Stimulants** (cocaine, amphetamines, methamphetamine, methylphenidate): Mechanisms of action, dopamine system effects
- **Dopamine agonists**: Direct receptor activation; therapeutic uses and effects
- **Dopamine precursors**: L-DOPA, precursor amino acids; bioavailability and effects
- **Reuptake inhibitors and modulators**: Effects on dopamine transporter and dopamine clearance

### Nutritional and Cofactor Support
- **Precursor amino acids**: Tyrosine, phenylalanine; synthesis, bioavailability, rationale
- **Cofactors**: BH4, B6, iron, magnesium; roles in dopamine synthesis
- **Antioxidants**: Vitamin C, E, N-acetylcysteine; oxidative stress and dopamine metabolism

### Neuromodulatory Compounds
- **Stimulants and adaptogens**: Caffeine, theanine, other compounds affecting dopamine
- **Herbal and botanical preparations**: Evidence for dopaminergic effects
- **Emerging compounds**: Experimental agents under investigation

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMERS

### Medical Information
This information is **for educational and informational purposes only**. It is **not medical advice** and should not be used to:
- Diagnose or treat any medical condition
- Replace consultation with a qualified healthcare provider
- Guide self-medication or supplement use
- Recommend dosing for any substance

### Safety
- All substances carry risks; benefits and risks vary by individual
- Interactions between substances, medications, and health conditions are possible and potentially serious
- Purity, potency, and contaminants in street drugs are unpredictable
- **Always consult a healthcare provider** before starting any new supplement, medication, or substance

## Substance Categories

### Abused Stimulants
Information on drugs of abuse is provided for educational understanding of mechanisms and effects, not as guidance for use.

### Prescription Medications
Information on FDA-approved medications is provided for understanding therapeutic mechanisms, not for self-treatment guidance.

### Over-the-Counter Supplements and Nutrients
Information on widely available compounds is provided to summarize evidence and mechanisms; clinical efficacy remains uncertain for most in withdrawal/recovery.
