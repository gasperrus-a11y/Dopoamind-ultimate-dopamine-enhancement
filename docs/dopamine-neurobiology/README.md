# Dopamine Neurobiology

This section provides comprehensive information about dopamine's biological mechanisms, including synthesis pathways, receptor systems, and neural circuits involved in reward and motivation.

## Contents

### Dopamine Synthesis and Metabolism
- **Overview**: The pathway from tyrosine to dopamine and its subsequent metabolism
- **Key Enzymes**: Tyrosine hydroxylase (TH), DOPA decarboxylase (DDC), monoamine oxidase (MAO), catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT)
- **Cofactors and Requirements**: Tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4), iron, and other essential factors

### Dopamine Receptors
- **D1-like receptors** (D1, D5): Gs-coupled, stimulatory
- **D2-like receptors** (D2, D3, D4): Gi/o-coupled, inhibitory
- **Receptor distribution** across brain regions
- **Signaling cascades** and downstream effects

### Dopamine Transporters and Reuptake
- **DAT (Dopamine Transporter)**: Primary mechanism for synaptic dopamine clearance
- **VMAT2 (Vesicular Monoamine Transporter 2)**: Packaging dopamine into vesicles
- **Reuptake kinetics** and factors affecting transporter function

### Neural Circuits and Pathways
- **Mesolimbic pathway**: Ventral tegmental area (VTA) to nucleus accumbens; reward and motivation
- **Mesocortical pathway**: VTA to prefrontal cortex; cognition and decision-making
- **Nigrostriatal pathway**: Substantia nigra to dorsal striatum; motor control
- **Tuberoinfundibular pathway**: Hypothalamus to pituitary; hormonal regulation

### Dopamine and Brain Function
- **Reward and pleasure**: Hedonic processing
- **Motivation and salience**: "Wanting" vs. "liking"
- **Learning and memory**: Reinforcement learning, habit formation
- **Executive function**: Attention, working memory, decision-making
- **Motor control**: Movement initiation and coordination
