# Dopoamind — Ultimate Dopamine Recovery & Reference

Dopamine recovery protocols and informational resources aimed at supporting recovery from stimulant withdrawal, traumatic brain injury–related changes, and anhedonia. This repository collects high-level summaries of relevant chemicals, pathways, and research references.

> IMPORTANT: This repository is for informational and educational purposes only. It is not medical advice. Always consult licensed medical professionals before trying any treatment, supplement, or protocol.

## Overview
This project gathers and organizes information about biological mechanisms and strategies that may be relevant to restoring dopaminergic function after prolonged stimulant use, brain injury, or other causes of anhedonia. The goal is to summarize scientific literature, list biochemical targets and relevant compounds, and provide a structured starting point for researchers, clinicians, and informed readers.

## Scope and limitations
- Focus: conceptual summaries of dopamine-related pathways, neurotransmitter interactions, and literature pointers.
- Excludes: individualized medical guidance, dosing protocols, or instructions for self-treatment.
- Intended audience: researchers, clinicians, students, and well-informed lay readers seeking references and high-level syntheses.

## Safety & disclaimer
This repository does NOT provide medical or clinical advice. Information about substances, supplements, or drugs can be dangerous if applied incorrectly. If you or someone else is experiencing severe withdrawal, psychiatric symptoms, or medical issues, contact emergency services or a qualified healthcare provider immediately.

## What’s in this repository
- Summaries of dopamine-related neurobiology (synthesis, receptors, transporters, degradation pathways).
- Literature notes on stimulant withdrawal and recovery from dopaminergic dysfunction.
- High-level descriptions of chemicals and classes (e.g., precursors, reuptake modulators, enzymatic modulators) and links to primary sources.
- Curated references and DOI/URL links to peer-reviewed studies and reviews.
- A contributors' guide for adding notes, references, or summaries.

## How to use this repository
- Read the overview and safety sections first.
- Use the references and links as entry points into the primary literature.
- Do not treat this repository as a treatment manual. Use it to inform research questions, literature reviews, or discussions with clinicians.

## Sources and references
This repository aims to cite peer-reviewed papers, review articles, and reputable textbooks. If you have key references to add, please open an issue or submit a pull request with full citation details (DOI, title, authors, and a short note about relevance).

## Contributing
Contributions are welcome. Please follow these guidelines:
1. Open an issue to propose a change or new content.
2. Fork the repository and create a branch for your changes.
3. Use clear commit messages and include references for factual claims.
4. Avoid prescriptive dosing or unverified clinical claims. Focus on summarizing peer-reviewed literature.

For substantial content changes, include citations (DOI or URL) and a short explanation of how the new material fits the repository’s scope.
