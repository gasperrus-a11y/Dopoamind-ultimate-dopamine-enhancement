### 1. Dopamine Neurobiology
*Understanding dopamine's biology, synthesis, receptors, and neural circuits.*

📁 **[docs/dopamine-neurobiology/](docs/dopamine-neurobiology/)**

**Key Files:**
- **[README.md](docs/dopamine-neurobiology/README.md)** — Overview and organization
- **[synthesis-and-metabolism.md](docs/dopamine-neurobiology/synthesis-and-metabolism.md)** — How dopamine is made and broken down
  - Dopamine synthesis pathway (tyrosine → L-DOPA → dopamine)
  - Synthesis regulation and cofactors
  - Storage and reuptake mechanisms
  - Metabolic pathways (MAO, COMT)
  - Factors affecting dopamine availability

- **[receptors.md](docs/dopamine-neurobiology/receptors.md)** — Dopamine receptor families and signaling
  - D1-like receptors (D1, D5): stimulatory
  - D2-like receptors (D2, D3, D4): inhibitory
  - Receptor distribution and functions
  - Downstream signaling cascades
  - Desensitization and adaptation
  - Changes in withdrawal and recovery

**Topics Covered:**
- Dopamine synthesis and metabolism
- Dopamine receptors (D1, D2, D3, D4, D5)
- Neural circuits and pathways (mesolimbic, mesocortical, nigrostriatal, tuberoinfundibular)
- Receptor signaling and downstream effects
- Adaptations to chronic drug exposure

**Best for:** Understanding how dopamine works biologically; foundation for all other sections
